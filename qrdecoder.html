How QR Code Decoding Works

QR (Quick Response) codes are 2D barcodes that store data in a matrix pattern of black and white squares. Each square represents binary information that scanners or cameras can read.

  • Step 1: The image is captured from your camera or uploaded from your device.
  • Step 2: The QR decoding library (jsQR) analyzes the image pixel-by-pixel.
  • Step 3: If a QR pattern is found, the encoded data (text, URL, etc.) is extracted.
  • Step 4: The decoded message is displayed in the result box, ready to copy or use.

QR codes are widely used for instant access to websites, apps, and secure data — without typing anything.
